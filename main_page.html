I started my journey from qbasic. It stared in the small computer lab at my old school where we didn't even have enough computers for everyone. 2 students shared one computer and someday if you are lucky then you were in a group of 3. I say "lucky" here because sharing one computer between you and your friend was one of the most memorable time of my school life. It was even more fun if we were in a group of 3.


I was a bit creative. I couldn't just stick to what the teacher was teaching. I wanted to try new things. When we started programming in Qbasic i figured I could make anything. Why stick to making a program for adding 2 numbers when you can make a full game. I first made a story based game where you made progress through making the right decision. Then I went a bit complex, I made Tic Tac Toe with no tutorials and no help just my mind and that computer screen. I then made a typing game where you had to type out a phrase in a certain time period. That game made a lot of my friends rage quit. It was so fun seeing my friends enjoy the game I made. That was when I discovered that this is what I wanted to do.
